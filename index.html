Research

My research is in computational topology and topological data analysis, with a focus on algorithms for persistent homology. I am particularly interested in combinatorial reductions of simplicial complexes and filtrations, such as edge collapses and strong collapses, that preserve relevant topological and persistence information.

More broadly, I work on problems at the interface of algebraic topology, computational geometry, and algorithms, especially where topological methods can be used to study large discrete or geometric data.

Keywords: computational topology, topological data analysis, persistent homology, simplicial complexes, filtered complexes, discrete Morse theory.
