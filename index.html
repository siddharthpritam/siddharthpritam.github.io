Research

My research is in computational topology and topological data analysis, with a focus on algorithms for persistent homology. A recurring theme in my work is the simplification of simplicial complexes and filtrations through combinatorial reductions, such as strong collapses and edge collapses, while preserving relevant topological and persistence information.

More broadly, I am interested in problems at the interface of algebraic topology, computational geometry, and theoretical computer science, especially where topological methods provide robust summaries of large discrete or geometric data.

Current directions include filtration-aware reductions, directed and multiparameter persistence, discrete Morse theoretic methods, and topological representations of graph-structured and temporal data.

Persistent homology

Algorithms, reductions, and structural questions for filtered complexes and graphs.

Collapses and reductions

Strong collapses, edge collapses, domination, and filtration-preserving simplifications.

Topological methods for data

Applications and foundations involving graphs, temporal data, and geometric complexes.
